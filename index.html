Verb Tenses — الأزمنة

الأزمنة هي قلب اللغة الإنجليزية. فهم متى تستخدم كل زمن هو مفتاح النجاح في الاختبار.

TenseFormulaKey Signal WordsExample
Present SimpleS + V(s/es)every day, always, usually, neverShe drives to work every day.
Past SimpleS + V-ed / irregularyesterday, last night, ago, in 2020They were at the cinema last night.
Present ContinuousS + am/is/are + V-ingnow, at the moment, currentlyHe is studying for the exam.
Past ContinuousS + was/were + V-ingwhen, while, at 5 o'clock yesterdayThey were having lunch when the phone rang.
Present PerfectS + have/has + V3already, yet, never, ever, just, since, forI have never seen such a sunset.
Past PerfectS + had + V3before, by the time, after, alreadyThey had finished before the deadline.
Present Perfect Cont.S + have/has been + V-ingfor, since + period of time (still ongoing)We have been studying for two years.
Future (going to)S + am/is/are going to + Vtomorrow, next week, plansThey are going to the zoo tomorrow.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • She go to school every day.
    ✓ She goes to school every day. (he/she/it + s)
  • I seen the movie already.
    ✓ I have seen the movie. (need have/has)
  • They was playing football.
    ✓ They were playing football. (plural → were)
  • She has went to Cairo.
    ✓ She has gone to Cairo. (V3 not V2)
  • I will call when I will get home.
    ✓ I will call when I get home. (present after when)

💡 Quick Rules

  • He / She / It → always add s or es in Present Simple
  • Use was for I/He/She/It, were for You/We/They in Past
  • Present Perfect uses have (I/you/we/they) or has (he/she/it)
  • Past Continuous: action was in progress when another happened
  • Use for with a duration, since with a start time
  • In time clauses (when/if), use Present Simple — not Future
  • Past Perfect = action done BEFORE another past action

Quantifiers — الكميات

Much / Many / Some / Any / A lot of — كل واحدة لها استخدام محدد حسب نوع الاسم والجملة.

QuantifierUse withSentence TypeExample
MUCHUncountable nouns❌ Negative / ❓ QuestionWe don't have much milk. / How much sugar?
MANYCountable plural nouns❌ Negative / ❓ QuestionI don't know many people. / How many books?
SOMEBoth types✅ Positive / OffersThere are some apples. / Would you like some?
ANYBoth types❌ Negative / ❓ QuestionWe don't have any milk. / Do you have any books?
A LOT OFBoth types✅ PositiveI have a lot of friends.

🔵 Countable vs Uncountable

  • Countable (عدّ) = books, friends, apples, people — يُجمع ✓
  • Uncountable (لا يُعدّ) = milk, water, money, sugar, time
  • Use MUCH only with uncountable: much water ✓ / much books ✗
  • Use MANY only with countable: many books ✓ / many water ✗
  • SOME in positive sentences, ANY in negatives/questions
  • Exception: Use SOME in questions when offering or requesting politely

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • I don't have many money.
    ✓ I don't have much money. (uncountable)
  • There are much apples.
    ✓ There are many apples. (countable)
  • I have some friends? (asking)
    ✓ Do you have any friends? (question → any)
  • There aren't some seats.
    ✓ There aren't any seats. (negative → any)

Comparatives & Superlatives — المقارنة

نستخدمها للمقارنة بين شخصين أو شيئين (comparative) أو ضمن مجموعة كاملة (superlative).

Adjective TypeComparativeSuperlativeExample
Short (1 syllable)
tall, long, fast, old		+ er + thanthe + esttaller than / the tallest
Ends in -y
easy, heavy, busy		change y→i + erchange y→i + esteasier / the easiest
Long (2+ syllables)
interesting, difficult, boring		more + adj + thanthe most + adjmore interesting / the most interesting
Irregular
good, bad, far		better / worse / fartherthe best / the worst / the farthestShe is the best student.

💡 Key Rules

  • Always use than after comparative: taller than her sister ✓
  • Never: more taller ✗ / more faster ✗ (double comparative!)
  • Superlative always needs the: the longest river ✓
  • good → better → the best (irregular!)
  • For adverbs: fast → faster, well → better

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • She is more taller than me.
    ✓ She is taller than me.
  • He is the more good student.
    ✓ He is the best student.
  • The Nile is most long river.
    ✓ The Nile is the longest river.
  • She is taller then her sister.
    ✓ She is taller than her sister. (than ≠ then)

Prepositions — حروف الجر

حروف الجر المكانية والزمانية — أكثر نقاط الاختبار التي يخطئ فيها الطلاب.

🕐 Time Prepositions

  • IN = months, years, seasons, parts of day: in January / in 2002 / in the morning
  • ON = specific days & dates: on Monday / on 5th May
  • AT = exact times & holidays: at 5 o'clock / at Christmas
  • FOR = duration (how long?): for two weeks / for five years
  • SINCE = start point (from when?): since Monday / since 2015
  • DURING = period when something happens: during the holiday

📌 Place Prepositions

  • IN = enclosed spaces, cities, countries: in the park / in Cairo
  • ON = surfaces: on the table / on the envelope
  • AT = specific points/locations: at the airport / at school
  • NEAR = close to: my house is near the bank
  • OVER = above/across: the plane flew over the city
  • UNDER = below: the cat is under the table
  • BY = transport method: by car / by train / by bus

arrive ___ the airportatSpecific location → at
born ___ January / ___ 1995inMonth / Year → in
stamp ___ the envelopeonSurface → on
go ___ car/bus/trainbyTransport → by
met ___ the parkinEnclosed outdoor area → in

Modal Verbs — أفعال الحالة

CAN / WOULD / COULD / MUST / SHOULD — أفعال مساعدة تعبر عن القدرة، الإذن، الطلب، والنصيحة.

ModalMain UsesExample
CANAbility / PermissionHe can speak three languages. / You can go home now.
COULDPast ability / Polite requestCould you help me, please?
WOULDPolite offers / requests, conditionalWould you like some tea? / Would she like to go?
MUSTObligation / Strong necessityYou must remember to put a stamp on it.
SHOULDAdvice / RecommendationYou should study harder.

💡 Key Rules

  • Modal verbs are ALWAYS followed by the base verb (infinitive without 'to')
  • He can speak ✓ (not speaks, not to speak)
  • No -s with he/she/it: He can ✓ / He cans ✗
  • Would you like = polite way to offer or invite
  • Would like = formal version of "want": I would like some tea
  • Can go ✓ / Can going ✗ / Can goes ✗

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • You can going home now.
    ✓ You can go home now.
  • He cans speak English.
    ✓ He can speak English. (no -s on modals)
  • Do you like some tea?
    ✓ Would you like some tea? (polite offer)
  • Would she likes to go?
    ✓ Would she like to go? (base verb after modal)
🏗️

Special Structures — تراكيب خاصة

SO / SUCH / ENOUGH / Tag Questions / Conjunctions — تراكيب لغوية متكررة في الاختبار.

SO vs SUCH

  • SO + adjective: The movie was so boring.
  • SUCH A + adj + noun: It was such a warm day.
  • SO...THAT: It was so warm that we went swimming.
  • SUCH A...THAT: It was such a warm day that we went swimming.
  • No article with SUCH + plural: such beautiful places ✓

ENOUGH Structure

  • Adjective + enough: brave enough / tall enough
  • NOT adjective + enough → not brave enough (إذا قبلها not)
  • Enough + noun: enough time / enough money
  • Enough comes AFTER adjectives but BEFORE nouns
  • Too + adjective (negative meaning): too tired to work

Tag Questions

  • Positive sentence + negative tag: She is tall, isn't she?
  • Negative sentence + positive tag: He isn't here, is he?
  • Let's go... → shall we?
  • Imperative → will you? / won't you?

Conjunctions — روابط الجمل

  • AND = addition: I like coffee and tea.
  • BUT = contrast: I like coffee but not tea.
  • SO = result/consequence: She was tired, so she went to bed.
  • BECAUSE = reason: She went to bed because she was tired.
  • SO vs BECAUSE: She was tired (because → reason) / She went to bed (so → result)

🔤 Determiners: THIS / THAT / THESE / THOSE

Near (قريب)Far (بعيد)
SingularTHIS boyTHAT girl
PluralTHESE booksTHOSE cars

Articles — أدوات التعريف والتنكير

A / AN / THE — من أكثر نقاط الاختبار التي يخطئ فيها الطلاب. القاعدة بسيطة إذا فهمت المنطق الأساسي.

ArticleWhen to UseExamples
ASingular countable noun — first mention — consonant sounda book, a car, a university (y-sound)
ANSingular countable noun — first mention — vowel sound (a, e, i, o, u)an apple, an egg, an hour (silent h)
THESpecific / unique / already mentioned / superlatives / with musical instrumentsthe sun, the capital city, the best student, play the piano
— (Zero)Plural general / Uncountable general / Languages / Sports / Meals— books are useful, — water, — English, — football, — breakfast

💡 Key Rules

  • A/AN = indefinite (any one thing). The = definite (a specific thing).
  • THE with unique nouns: the sun, the moon, the Nile, the capital city
  • THE with superlatives: the best, the longest, the most expensive
  • A/AN based on SOUND, not spelling: an hour (silent h), a university (sounds like "yoo")
  • Second mention → THE: "I saw a dog. The dog was big."
  • Play + THE + instrument: play the piano, the guitar ✓
  • Play + — + sport: play football, play tennis ✓ (no article)

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • What is a capital city of Japan?
    ✓ What is the capital city? (unique → the)
  • She plays a piano very well.
    ✓ She plays the piano. (instrument → the)
  • I need a umbrella.
    ✓ I need an umbrella. (vowel sound → an)
  • The exhibition was awful. (first mention)
    ✓ Use The only when the listener already knows which one (museum visit already happened → the exhibition).

Conditional Sentences — الجمل الشرطية

If-clauses تعبر عن الشروط والنتائج. الفرق بين نوعها هو الفرق بين الحقيقي والافتراضي.

TypeIf-ClauseMain ClauseMeaningExample
ZeroPresent SimplePresent SimpleAlways true / factsIf you heat ice, it melts.
FirstPresent Simplewill + base verbReal / possible futureIf it rains, we will stay home.
SecondPast Simple (were for all)would + base verbUnreal / hypothetical presentIf I were you, I would study harder.

💡 Key Rules

  • NEVER use 'will' inside the if-clause of First Conditional
  • If it rains (not 'will rain'), we will go inside.
  • In Second Conditional, use were for all subjects: If I were / If she were
  • The if-clause can come first OR second: If I study, I'll pass ✓ / I'll pass if I study ✓
  • In case ≠ if. "Take an umbrella in case it rains" = as a precaution (not a condition)
  • First Conditional: real, likely situations. Second: impossible or unlikely.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • If you will give us a discount, we will consider.
    ✓ If you give us a discount, we will consider. (present simple in if-clause)
  • If I was you, I would apologize.
    ✓ If I were you... (were for all persons in 2nd conditional)
  • She would call if she knows the answer.
    ✓ She would call if she knew the answer. (past simple in 2nd cond.)
  • If you gave / will consider (mixed)
    ✓ If you give / will consider (consistent 1st conditional)

📝 Quick Reference Examples from the Exam

If I don't eat breakfast, I will be hungry around 10:00.
First Conditional: Present Simple (don't) + will (will be)
If you give us a 5% discount, we will consider your proposal.
First Conditional: Business negotiation — real/possible condition
Andrew carries a card in case he doesn't have enough cash.
In case = as a precaution (different from if)

Phrasal Verbs — الأفعال المركبة

Verb + Particle(s) — الأفعال التي تتغير معانيها عند إضافة حرف جر أو ظرف مكان إليها.

📋 High-Frequency Phrasal Verbs

  • Sign up for = to enroll/register: sign up for tennis lessons ✓ (must use 'for' with object)
  • Come up = something unexpected happens: "Something has come up" = a problem arose
  • Check in = register arrival: check in at the office / hotel
  • Check out = leave hotel / investigate: check out of the hotel
  • Look at = examine/view: How many apartments have they looked at?
  • Out of = from inside: take the key out of my bag
  • Fill in / fill out = complete a form
  • Call off = cancel: The meeting was called off.
  • Turn off = stop: Turn off the lights.
  • Run into = meet unexpectedly: I ran into an old friend.

⚠️ Tricky Pairs to Remember

  • Would you like to sign up tennis lessons?
    ✓ Sign up for tennis lessons. (needs 'for' before the object)
  • Something has come over. (= unexpected)
    ✓ Something has come up. (come over = visit someone's place)
  • I have to check to at the office.
    ✓ I have to check in at the office. (register/report presence)
  • Take the key up my bag.
    ✓ Take the key out of my bag. (movement from inside)

🔤 Attend vs Visit vs See vs Participate

VerbCollocates WithExample
ATTENDevents, receptions, meetings, conferences, classesattend a reception / attend a meeting ✓
VISITplaces, people, websitesvisit a museum / visit a friend ✓
SEEshows, performances, doctorssee a movie / see a doctor ✓
PARTICIPATEalways needs IN: participate inparticipate in an event ✓ (not "participate a reception")

Adverbs & Vocabulary — الظروف والمفردات

Adverbs of manner (slowly, carefully) + Frequency (always, rarely) + Key vocabulary tested in the exam.

📊 Frequency Adverbs (High → Low)

  • always = 100%: He always arrives on time.
  • usually / generally ≈ 80%: She usually walks to work.
  • often / frequently ≈ 60%: They often go to the cinema.
  • sometimes ≈ 40%: I sometimes have tea.
  • rarely / seldom ≈ 10%: We rarely see him these days.
  • never = 0%: She never eats meat.
  • Position: before main verb, after be/auxiliaries: He is always late / She rarely goes.

🔤 Manner Adverbs (Adj → Adverb)

  • Most: add -ly: slow → slowly, careful → carefully, quiet → quietly
  • Ends in -y: change to -ily: happy → happily, easy → easily
  • Irregular: good → well, fast → fast (no change), hard → hard
  • Adverbs answer "HOW?": Drive slowly. Speak quietly. Work hard.
  • ADJECTIVE modifies nouns; ADVERB modifies verbs: a slow car ✓ / drive slowly ✓

Passive Voice — المبني للمجهول

نستخدم المبني للمجهول عندما لا نعرف من فعل الشيء، أو عندما يكون الشيء المفعول به أهم من الفاعل.

TenseActivePassive FormulaPassive Example
Present SimplePeople speak English everywhere.am/is/are + V3English is spoken everywhere.
Past SimpleSomeone broke the window.was/were + V3The window was broken.
Present PerfectThey have canceled the meeting.has/have been + V3The meeting has been canceled.
Future (will)They will announce the results.will be + V3The results will be announced.
Modal + PassiveThey may increase the price.may/might + be + V3The price may be increased.

💡 Key Rules

  • Passive formula: be (correct form) + V3
  • Use by to mention the agent: The letter was written by Sarah.
  • Modal passive: may/might/could + be + V3: The price may be increased.
  • T-shirts are made of cotton. (made of = material it's made from)
  • Use passive when the agent is unknown, unimportant, or obvious.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • The price might increased next month.
    ✓ The price might be increased. (modal + be + V3)
  • English is spoke everywhere.
    ✓ English is spoken. (V3, not V2)
  • The letter was write yesterday.
    ✓ The letter was written. (V3: write → written)
  • T-shirts are made from cotton.
    ✓ Made of cotton (material). Made from = process changes shape (wine is made from grapes).
