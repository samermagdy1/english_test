Verb Tenses — الأزمنة

الأزمنة هي قلب اللغة الإنجليزية. فهم متى تستخدم كل زمن هو مفتاح النجاح في الاختبار.

TenseFormulaKey Signal WordsExample
Present SimpleS + V(s/es)every day, always, usually, neverShe drives to work every day.
Past SimpleS + V-ed / irregularyesterday, last night, ago, in 2020They were at the cinema last night.
Present ContinuousS + am/is/are + V-ingnow, at the moment, currentlyHe is studying for the exam.
Past ContinuousS + was/were + V-ingwhen, while, at 5 o'clock yesterdayThey were having lunch when the phone rang.
Present PerfectS + have/has + V3already, yet, never, ever, just, since, forI have never seen such a sunset.
Past PerfectS + had + V3before, by the time, after, alreadyThey had finished before the deadline.
Present Perfect Cont.S + have/has been + V-ingfor, since + period of time (still ongoing)We have been studying for two years.
Future (going to)S + am/is/are going to + Vtomorrow, next week, plansThey are going to the zoo tomorrow.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • She go to school every day.
    ✓ She goes to school every day. (he/she/it + s)
  • I seen the movie already.
    ✓ I have seen the movie. (need have/has)
  • They was playing football.
    ✓ They were playing football. (plural → were)
  • She has went to Cairo.
    ✓ She has gone to Cairo. (V3 not V2)
  • I will call when I will get home.
    ✓ I will call when I get home. (present after when)

💡 Quick Rules

  • He / She / It → always add s or es in Present Simple
  • Use was for I/He/She/It, were for You/We/They in Past
  • Present Perfect uses have (I/you/we/they) or has (he/she/it)
  • Past Continuous: action was in progress when another happened
  • Use for with a duration, since with a start time
  • In time clauses (when/if), use Present Simple — not Future
  • Past Perfect = action done BEFORE another past action

Quantifiers — الكميات

Much / Many / Some / Any / A lot of — كل واحدة لها استخدام محدد حسب نوع الاسم والجملة.

QuantifierUse withSentence TypeExample
MUCHUncountable nouns❌ Negative / ❓ QuestionWe don't have much milk. / How much sugar?
MANYCountable plural nouns❌ Negative / ❓ QuestionI don't know many people. / How many books?
SOMEBoth types✅ Positive / OffersThere are some apples. / Would you like some?
ANYBoth types❌ Negative / ❓ QuestionWe don't have any milk. / Do you have any books?
A LOT OFBoth types✅ PositiveI have a lot of friends.

🔵 Countable vs Uncountable

  • Countable (عدّ) = books, friends, apples, people — يُجمع ✓
  • Uncountable (لا يُعدّ) = milk, water, money, sugar, time
  • Use MUCH only with uncountable: much water ✓ / much books ✗
  • Use MANY only with countable: many books ✓ / many water ✗
  • SOME in positive sentences, ANY in negatives/questions
  • Exception: Use SOME in questions when offering or requesting politely

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • I don't have many money.
    ✓ I don't have much money. (uncountable)
  • There are much apples.
    ✓ There are many apples. (countable)
  • I have some friends? (asking)
    ✓ Do you have any friends? (question → any)
  • There aren't some seats.
    ✓ There aren't any seats. (negative → any)

Comparatives & Superlatives — المقارنة

نستخدمها للمقارنة بين شخصين أو شيئين (comparative) أو ضمن مجموعة كاملة (superlative).

Adjective TypeComparativeSuperlativeExample
Short (1 syllable)
tall, long, fast, old		+ er + thanthe + esttaller than / the tallest
Ends in -y
easy, heavy, busy		change y→i + erchange y→i + esteasier / the easiest
Long (2+ syllables)
interesting, difficult, boring		more + adj + thanthe most + adjmore interesting / the most interesting
Irregular
good, bad, far		better / worse / fartherthe best / the worst / the farthestShe is the best student.

💡 Key Rules

  • Always use than after comparative: taller than her sister ✓
  • Never: more taller ✗ / more faster ✗ (double comparative!)
  • Superlative always needs the: the longest river ✓
  • good → better → the best (irregular!)
  • For adverbs: fast → faster, well → better

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • She is more taller than me.
    ✓ She is taller than me.
  • He is the more good student.
    ✓ He is the best student.
  • The Nile is most long river.
    ✓ The Nile is the longest river.
  • She is taller then her sister.
    ✓ She is taller than her sister. (than ≠ then)

Prepositions — حروف الجر

حروف الجر المكانية والزمانية — أكثر نقاط الاختبار التي يخطئ فيها الطلاب.

🕐 Time Prepositions

  • IN = months, years, seasons, parts of day: in January / in 2002 / in the morning
  • ON = specific days & dates: on Monday / on 5th May
  • AT = exact times & holidays: at 5 o'clock / at Christmas
  • FOR = duration (how long?): for two weeks / for five years
  • SINCE = start point (from when?): since Monday / since 2015
  • DURING = period when something happens: during the holiday

📌 Place Prepositions

  • IN = enclosed spaces, cities, countries: in the park / in Cairo
  • ON = surfaces: on the table / on the envelope
  • AT = specific points/locations: at the airport / at school
  • NEAR = close to: my house is near the bank
  • OVER = above/across: the plane flew over the city
  • UNDER = below: the cat is under the table
  • BY = transport method: by car / by train / by bus

Modal Verbs — أفعال الحالة

CAN / WOULD / COULD / MUST / SHOULD — أفعال مساعدة تعبر عن القدرة، الإذن، الطلب، والنصيحة.

ModalMain UsesExample
CANAbility / PermissionHe can speak three languages. / You can go home now.
COULDPast ability / Polite requestCould you help me, please?
WOULDPolite offers / requests, conditionalWould you like some tea? / Would she like to go?
MUSTObligation / Strong necessityYou must remember to put a stamp on it.
SHOULDAdvice / RecommendationYou should study harder.

💡 Key Rules

  • Modal verbs are ALWAYS followed by the base verb (infinitive without 'to')
  • He can speak ✓ (not speaks, not to speak)
  • No -s with he/she/it: He can ✓ / He cans ✗
  • Would you like = polite way to offer or invite
  • Would like = formal version of "want": I would like some tea
  • Can go ✓ / Can going ✗ / Can goes ✗

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • You can going home now.
    ✓ You can go home now.
  • He cans speak English.
    ✓ He can speak English. (no -s on modals)
  • Do you like some tea?
    ✓ Would you like some tea? (polite offer)
  • Would she likes to go?
    ✓ Would she like to go? (base verb after modal)
🏗️

Special Structures — تراكيب خاصة

SO / SUCH / ENOUGH / Tag Questions / Conjunctions — تراكيب لغوية متكررة في الاختبار.

SO vs SUCH

  • SO + adjective: The movie was so boring.
  • SUCH A + adj + noun: It was such a warm day.
  • SO...THAT: It was so warm that we went swimming.
  • SUCH A...THAT: It was such a warm day that we went swimming.
  • No article with SUCH + plural: such beautiful places ✓

ENOUGH Structure

  • Adjective + enough: brave enough / tall enough
  • NOT adjective + enough → not brave enough (إذا قبلها not)
  • Enough + noun: enough time / enough money
  • Enough comes AFTER adjectives but BEFORE nouns
  • Too + adjective (negative meaning): too tired to work

Tag Questions

  • Positive sentence + negative tag: She is tall, isn't she?
  • Negative sentence + positive tag: He isn't here, is he?
  • Let's go... → shall we?
  • Imperative → will you? / won't you?

Conjunctions — روابط الجمل

  • AND = addition: I like coffee and tea.
  • BUT = contrast: I like coffee but not tea.
  • SO = result/consequence: She was tired, so she went to bed.
  • BECAUSE = reason: She went to bed because she was tired.
  • SO vs BECAUSE: She was tired (because → reason) / She went to bed (so → result)
